## Preventing Future Regrets: Preconception Care for Women in Their 20s and 30s

As women navigate the diverse lifestyles and choices of modern society in their 20s and 30s, it's not uncommon to feel a mix of excitement for the future and vague anxieties. We, as obstetricians and gynecologists, frequently hear concerns like, "I want children someday, but how will I balance it with my career?" or "If I marry later, will I still be able to get pregnant?"

To address these future uncertainties and empower you to confidently choose the life you desire, the concept of **"preconception care"** is gaining global attention. This refers to the practice of individuals and their partners focusing on their physical and mental well-being even before considering pregnancy. If this sounds new to you, understand that it's not a directive to "get pregnant now." Rather, it is an **"investment in yourself"** to broaden your future options and build a richer life.

### What is Preconception Care? And Why Do We Need It Now?

Preconception care is a comprehensive health management approach for individuals or couples who desire pregnancy, or are considering it in the future, to review their health status before pregnancy. By adopting a healthier lifestyle, they prepare for a healthy pregnancy and childbirth, and also **protect the health of their future child**. This is a crucial modern healthcare strategy, recommended by the World Health Organization (WHO).

Why is this approach so vital for us today?

1.  **Trends of Later Marriages and Childbirths:** With women's increasing roles in society, marriage and childbirth ages are rising, often coinciding with career development. It's important to consider that fertility may decline with age, and the risks associated with pregnancy and childbirth may increase.
2.  **Increase in Lifestyle-Related Diseases:** Even among younger generations, changes in diet and lack of exercise contribute to a rise in lifestyle-related diseases. These can increase the risk of complications during pregnancy.
3.  **Information Overload and Misinformation:** The internet is flooded with information, much of which lacks scientific basis. Obtaining accurate knowledge from specialists is crucial.

Preconception care is not only about the major life event of pregnancy and childbirth but also serves as a foundation for **extending a woman's healthy lifespan and improving her Quality of Life (QOL)**. It transforms vague anxieties about the future into confidence through accurate knowledge and proactive steps.

### What You Can Do Now! Concrete Steps for Preconception Care

If you're wondering, "Where do I start?", rest assured it's not complicated. Begin with what you can consciously incorporate into your daily life.

#### 1. Healthy Eating Habits and Supplement Use

*   **Balanced Diet:** Consume a balanced diet of vegetables, fruits, grains, and protein. Limit processed foods and excessive sugar intake.
*   **Folic Acid Intake:** For women planning pregnancy, **folic acid is a critically important nutrient**. Supplementing with folic acid for at least one month before conception and throughout early pregnancy is recommended to reduce the risk of neural tube defects. [Learn more about our clinic's approach to preconception health](/en).

#### 2. Regular Exercise and Maintaining a Healthy Weight

*   **Exercise Routine:** Incorporate walking or light aerobic exercise several times a week. Being overweight or underweight can affect fertility.
*   **BMI Management:** The ideal Body Mass Index (BMI) is between 18.5 and 24.9. Understand your BMI and strive to maintain it within a healthy range.

#### 3. Stress Management and Sufficient Sleep

*   **Time for Relaxation:** Stress can impact hormonal balance. Make time for hobbies, meditation, or other activities that relax your mind and body.
*   **Quality Sleep:** Regular, quality sleep is essential for bodily recovery and maintaining hormonal balance.

#### 4. Quitting Smoking and Moderating Alcohol Intake

*   **No Smoking:** Smoking negatively affects reproductive function in both men and women and increases risks for pregnancy complications and the baby. Quitting smoking is one of the most critical aspects of preconception care.
*   **Moderate Alcohol Consumption:** Alcohol consumption can also affect fetal development. If you are trying to conceive, consider abstaining or significantly reducing your intake.

#### 5. Regular Health Check-ups and Gynecological Exams

*   **Regular Checks:** In addition to general health check-ups, undergo regular gynecological exams (e.g., cervical cancer screening) to understand your body's condition. Screening for sexually transmitted infections is also important.
*   **Managing Chronic Conditions:** If you have chronic conditions such as diabetes or thyroid disorders, consult with a specialist before pregnancy to ensure they are well-controlled.

#### 6. Vaccinations

*   **Rubella Antibody Test and Vaccine:** Rubella infection during pregnancy can cause severe birth defects in the baby. Get tested for rubella antibodies before pregnancy, and consider vaccination if your immunity is insufficient.

### Partners Too! Preconception Care as a Couple's Journey

Preconception care is not solely a woman's responsibility. When partners engage together, its effectiveness significantly increases. A man's health status (smoking, alcohol, obesity, chronic conditions) is known to affect sperm quality and future pregnancy outcomes. We encourage you to discuss this with your partner and take steps together toward a healthy future.

### Knowledge as Self-Investment, Expanding Future Choices

Perhaps you thought, "I'm not planning to get pregnant yet, so this doesn't concern me." However, understanding your body and adopting healthy lifestyle habits will empower you, regardless of your future choices. Whether it's pursuing your career, building a healthy family, or simply enhancing your own QOL, this knowledge is invaluable.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1: When should I start preconception care?**

A1: Ideally, you should start preconception care **when you begin to consider pregnancy**, or even **from your 20s or 30s if you might wish to become pregnant in the future**. There is no fixed time, but the earlier you start, the more systematically you can work towards building a healthy body. Even starting a few months before conception is known to have significant benefits.

**Q2: Should my partner also participate in preconception care?**

A2: Yes, it is strongly recommended. Preconception care is based on the idea that **the health of both partners deeply impacts the success of a pregnancy and the health of the child**. A man's lifestyle habits (smoking, alcohol, diet, etc.) and health status can affect sperm quality. Moreover, as partners who will share in raising a child, building a healthy lifestyle together is profoundly important.
