## What is Preconception Care? A Health Investment for Your Future Self, Starting Today

"If I decide to have a baby in the future, will I be healthy enough to welcome it?"

"I want to value my career, but I'm worried about the timing with childbirth..."

Many women in their 20s and 30s likely harbor such vague anxieties about the future. If this sounds like you, then preconception care is something you should definitely know about. This refers to efforts made by women and couples to adopt healthy lifestyle habits to prepare for future pregnancy and childbirth, regardless of whether they are actively planning to conceive. The World Health Organization (WHO) and other public health bodies like the CDC emphasize its importance.

Preconception care is not simply "preparation for pregnancy." Rather, it is the best self-investment to expand your life choices and design a richer future.

## Why is Preconception Care Important Now?

### It Leads to Lifelong Health, Not Just Pregnancy and Childbirth

The healthy lifestyle habits you develop through preconception care will have a positive impact not only during pregnancy but also on your lifelong health. You can expect various benefits, including the prevention of lifestyle-related diseases, stable hormone balance, and improved mental health.

### Preparing for the "What If" of Pregnancy

While some pregnancies proceed as planned, others may occur unexpectedly. Maintaining a healthy state generally helps reduce risks for both you and your baby in such situations. For instance, as late marriage and childbearing become more common, especially in Japan, managing your health from a younger age is crucial to mitigate risks associated with advanced maternal age (e.g., gestational hypertension, gestational diabetes) as much as possible.

### Care to Consider with Your Partner

Preconception care is not solely a woman's concern. The health status of the male partner also influences a couple's chances of conception and the health of their future child. By considering health together and supporting each other, you can build a better future.

## Steps You Can Take Today for Preconception Care

"So, what specifically should I do?" you might be wondering. There's no need to overcomplicate it. Let's start with small steps.

### Start by "Knowing Yourself"

*   Undergo Health Check-ups: This is the first step to understanding your current physical condition. Any abnormalities can be addressed early.
*   Record Your Menstrual Cycle: Tracking your body's rhythm, whether by measuring basal body temperature or using an app, is important.
*   Understand Your Genetic Background: Knowing your family's medical history can help you prepare for future risks.

### Review Daily Lifestyle Habits

*   Balanced Diet: Folic acid is a particularly important nutrient before and during pregnancy. Make sure to consume it consciously as it helps reduce the risk of neural tube defects. It's also vital to get a balanced intake of other vitamins and minerals.
    [Discover the importance of folic acid](/en/blog/the-importance-of-folic-acid)
*   Regular Exercise: Incorporate sustainable exercises like walking or yoga a few times a week. This also helps reduce stress and manage weight.
*   Quality Sleep: Sufficient sleep is essential for balancing hormones and physical and mental recovery.
*   Avoid Smoking and Limit Alcohol: Smoking and excessive alcohol consumption negatively impact your overall health, not just future pregnancy and childbirth. It's advisable to limit them as much as possible.

### Prioritize Mental Well-being

Stress can disrupt your physical and mental balance. Manage stress in a way that suits you, such as engaging in hobbies, talking to trusted individuals, or incorporating meditation.

### The Importance of Having a Family Doctor

Having a place where you can seek advice when needed brings peace of mind. It is recommended to find a family doctor you feel comfortable consulting.

## Expand Your Future Options, Starting Today

Preconception care is by no means an "obligation." It is an "investment" to design your future more freely and authentically. The small steps you take today will undoubtedly lead to a brilliant future. Without rushing, at your own pace, why not start investing in your health now?

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: When should I start preconception care?
A1: Regardless of whether you are planning to conceive, any woman of reproductive age should consider starting preconception care. The earlier you begin, the greater the potential benefits. Specifically, starting several months before desired conception allows for more planned preparation.

Q2: Is preconception care only for women?
A2: No, it applies to male partners as well. A man's lifestyle and health status also affect the chances of conception and the health of the child. When couples engage in it together, more effective care can be expected.
